All About Me - Alayna Daws

Introduction

Hi, my name is Alayna Daws. In August 2023, I completed a Master of Science degree in Statistics from the University of Georgia (UGA). I completed my undergraduate degree in statistics at UGA in May 2022. I am a third-generation UGA graduate and believe my blood truly runs red and black. I am currently seeking employment and am most looking forward to gaining additional experience in the real-world with messy data.

Why Statistics?

My path to statistics has been a little unconventional. I began college pursuing an education in microbiology with the goal to become a virologist. During my first semester at UGA, I took a biostatistics course. This course introduced me to a profession that combined my love for science, love for math, and desire to help others. My first biostatistics professor encouraged me to add a statistics minor, so I added a minor in statistics that evening. Since then, my statistics minor has evolved into a statistics major. Statistics has empowered me to better contribute to research in every stage of the scientific process from experimental design to exploratory data analysis. I love that statistics grants me the ability to “play in everyone’s backyard” (John Tukey)!

Background

Firefly Partners, LLC project is women-owned and delivers solutions that help progressive nonprofits achieve goals. This partnership was facilitated through Riipen.
  • I have leadership and time management experience through my roles as a Resident Assistant and as the Vice Chair of Assignments of the Arch Society.

Bioinformatics Tutorial

Building a Phylogeny with R

I have attached a link to a tutorial of gathering sequence data, reading the sequence data into R, and creating phylogenetic trees. There are multiple approaches to complete this. The video uses a slighlty different approach I have used in the past. Last Spring, I completed a project using COVID-19 sequence data to create and analyze phylogenetic trees. The R package “phangorn” was used to fit phylogenetic trees from aligned sequence data. The sequence data was imported using the “read.phydat” command as a FASTA file. A distance matrix was calculated using the “dist.ml” function. Distance-based methods result in a transformation of the sequence data into pairwise distances. I really enjoyed this project and wanted to share some information about Phylogeny in R.
